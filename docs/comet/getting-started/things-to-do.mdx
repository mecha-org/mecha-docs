The Mecha Comet is a modular handheld Linux device designed for flexibility and functionality. Below is a guide to routine tasks and common operations to make the most of your device.

---

### 1. **Powering On and Off**

- **To Power On:**
    1. Press and hold the power button for 2 seconds.
    2. Wait for the Mechanix OS to boot.
- **To Power Off:**
    1. Long-press the power button.
    2. OR there is a provision in GUI, in notification bar. 

### 2. **Connecting to Wi-Fi**

1. Open the **Settings** app.
2. Navigate to **Network** > **Wi-Fi**.
3. Select your preferred network and enter the password.
4. Confirm the connection.

### 3. **Installing Applications**

- Use the **Mechanix App Store** or terminal commands:
    
    ```
    sudo apt update
    sudo apt install <package-name>
    ```
    

### 4. **Using Extensions**

- **Gamepad Extension:** Attach to the modular port for gaming. Configure controls in the **Gaming Settings**.
- **GPIO Extension:** Use for hardware prototyping. Pin mappings are accessible through the **Mechanix GPIO Manager**.
- **Keyboard Extension:** Plug and play for text input.

### 5. **Adjusting Display Settings**

1. Go to **Settings** > **Display**.
2. Adjust brightness, resolution, and orientation.

### 6. **Adjusting Sound Settings**

1. Go to **Settings** > **Sound**.
2. Adjust volume.

### 7. **Customizing the GUI**

- Access the **Mechanix Shell Settings** to:
    - Change themes.
    - Modify application shortcuts.
    - Enable/disable widgets.

### 8. **Managing Files**

1. Open the **File Manager** from the home screen.
2. Browse, copy, move, or delete files.
3. Use a USB connection to transfer files to/from a computer.

### 9. **Connecting External Devices**

- Use USB or the M.2 slot for:
    - Storage drives.
    - AI accelerators.
- For Raspberry Pi HATs and MikroE Click Boards, align pins carefully and secure the connection.

### 10. **Backup and Restore**

- **Backup:**
    
    ```
    sudo tar -cvpzf backup.tar.gz /path/to/backup
    ```
    
- **Restore:**
    
    ```
    sudo tar -xvpzf backup.tar.gz -C /
    ```
    

### 12. **Troubleshooting Common Issues**

- **Device Freezing:** Long-press the power button to restart.
- **Wi-Fi Not Connecting:**
    - Ensure the network is active.
    - Restart the device.
    - Run: `sudo systemctl restart NetworkManager`.
- **Unresponsive Extensions:** Reattach and verify connections.
